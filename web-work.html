Dapper

My latest project, this site is inspired by my passion for men’s clothing and fashion. It is my first site that I have used Compass and Google Maps since learning them. I created the logos using Inkscape and saved them as SVG’s for better scalability. It uses many pictures and tries to be as minimal as possible while still being modern. Now, many of my sites expand the whole viewport, however with this site I constrained myself to a container and to stay inside it. Challenging myself to use a good portion of whitespace. My favorite part of this site is the product section as once it is clicked creates a modal that slides down. From there, whenever you hover over a list item it shows a picture on the right side of that particular product. I created two different navigation menus, one that will display the logo at the top of navigation in mobile view and the other for medium viewports which display the logo in the middle of the navigation. The site is almost complete and will be updated once 100% completely finished. As always, I started the project by completing sketches and thinking about what I would like the site to look like. It uses the font Josefin Sans.

Made with:

  • HTML,
  • CSS,
  • SASS,
  • Compass,
  • ZURB Foundation,
  • JavaScript,
  • jQuery,
  • Google Maps

Aerom

This site is a mock website that I created due to my interest in journals and writing. The backgrounds for the header section and footer section challenged me to think outside the box and find a way to create the slanted effect, which I achieved using borders. My process for the site was to start a sketch in my notebook, from there experiment with different elements and find a layout that would be modern but also very minimal. After I finding the right combo I created a mockup in Photoshop and used it to guide me. I experimented with a serif font as the main font on this page I concluded that Butler, downloaded from the internet would be the best fit matched with Fira Sans. The photos used are not mine and were found on Unsplash.

Digim Marketing

A marketing website that I created when first learning Bootstrap and jQuery. It uses the background-attachment: fixed CSS property and value to create a parallax effect. As with all my projects, I start out with a vision and communicate that in a sketchbook. I sketched out different ideas and layouts for the site in various forms, such as mobile, tablet, and desktop. With the contact section, taking advantage of modals, I used my knowledge of Bootstrap modals and allowed the contact section to be a modal. When you click on it, it will pop up a contact form instead of going to a different page. The name Digim is just something that I thought of while designing the site and just came to my head and stuck with it. It uses a lot of Bootstraps glyph icons and photos from Unsplash, while also using only one font throughout the whole site, the google font “Mallanna”.

Made with:

  • HTML,
  • CSS,
  • SASS,
  • jQuery,
  • Twitter Bootstrap
